Hey, I’m Bryant

I'm currently the Head of Machine Learning at Addavox with a passion for machine learning and natural language processing. I love building things that make life easier and more efficient, especially for inter-lingual communication.

I recently graduated with my Master's of Computer Science at Texas A&M University. My research projects concentrated on cutting-edge Machine Learning algorithms. My final paper, HFMF: Hierarchical Fusion Meets Multi-Stream Models for Deepfake Detection was accepted to IEEE/CVF WACV 2025 Workshop where I presented it. 

I served a volunteer mission for The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints in Poland for 25 months. I learned to read, write and speak Polish. After returning home to my studies at Brigham Young University I became more interested in linguistics. I refined my 4 years of high school Spanish, took another Polish class and began learning Russian. I found the intersection of my interests lied in Computational Linguistics, specifically Natural Language Processing (NLP) and Machine Translation. In 2023, I graduated with my BS from BYU in Applied and Computational Mathematics (ACME) with an emphasis in Linguistics. 

I am now married to my beautiful wife Taylor, we have two boys named Cohen and Milo. Outside of spending time with my family I am still always working on my own projects, some of which I will post here.

Back in the day, I helped start a small private consulting gig called Parsha Works. My three partners and I have since moved on to bigger and better things. However, feel free to contact me for any questions, help on projects, or career opportunities.
